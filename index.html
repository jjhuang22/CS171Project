chapter 3: journey's end?

What is success?

Generally, venture-funded startups have two exit options once they mature:

go public or sell.

When a company goes public, or IPOs, it makes a splash in the business world. From 2007 to 2013,

the average IPO-bound startup raised $162 million in funding for a
$467.9 million IPO.

Notable IPOs include Facebook in 2012 and Twitter in 2013.

Getting acquired is less flashy but fills pockets nonetheless:

the average successfully acquired startup raised $29.4 million and sold for $155.5 million.

Examples include YouTube, which was acquired by Google.

As you might expect, a startup's fundraising success often provides clues about how successful they are upon exit. Here, we look at funding and acquisition data from companies that were acquired between 2007 and 2014.
