chapter 2: baby steps

The world of startups is notoriously cutthroat. To survive and grow, a startup needs capital. Where does it come from?

First, let’s clear up some vocab.

It is common for startups to pursue venture capital to grow their business. The earliest stage often consists of

seed funding or angel investors,

whose money goes toward building the product and proving that the idea is worth pursuing.

Series A funding

(which often lasts for 18-24 months) helps a company scale its operations and minimize risk. Finally,

Series B and C funding

is when the company expands, utilizing the growth machinery that it created during Series A. By the time a company reaches Series B, it is already well-established; by the time it reaches Series C, it can be termed quite successful.

What is success?

Generally, venture-funded startups have two exit options once they mature:

go public or sell.

When a company goes public, or IPOs, it makes a splash in the business world. (have headlines zoom in on parallax in the background) From 2007 to 2013,
